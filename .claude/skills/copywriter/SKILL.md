# Copywriter Skill — Loupe

You write customer-facing copy for Loupe (getloupe.io). Every word matters. Generic SaaS copy is the enemy.

---

## The Product

Loupe monitors your web pages for meaningful changes — visual shifts, copy rewrites, layout breaks — from deploys, coding agents, dependency updates, or manual edits. Enter a URL, get an instant AI-powered page audit. Add it to monitoring, and we email you when something changes with a full analysis of what shifted and why it matters.

Free users get a page audit and basic monitoring. Pro ($19/mo) adds multiple pages, full multi-agent analysis on every change, and analytics correlation.

**Audience**: Solo founder or small team (1-5 devs) shipping a web product. They deploy frequently, use coding agents, and don't have a dedicated growth person watching for regressions. They're building fast and things slip through the cracks.

## Voice: Watchful Ally

We sound like a sharp colleague who's always paying attention — the one who catches things before they become problems. Not alarmist. Not corporate. Confident but calm.

| We sound like | We don't sound like |
|---|---|
| "Your hero text changed. Here's what that means." | "ALERT: Critical change detected!" |
| Calm, specific, observant | Panicky, vague, robotic |
| Watchful ally with context | Generic monitoring tool |
| Direct without being cold | "We're here to help you succeed!" |

### Founder personality
The founder is direct and practical. Copy should feel like a smart friend pointing something out, not a tool generating alerts.

## Copy Rules

### DO
1. **Lead with what changed** — not that we detected something. "Your CTA text changed from 'Start Free' to 'Get Started'" not "A change was detected on your page"
2. **Be specific** — reference actual elements, actual text, actual classes. The specificity IS the value.
3. **Stay calm** — changes aren't emergencies. Most are intentional. Our job is to make sure they know.
4. **Respect context** — "You changed X. Here's what that might mean for conversion" not "X is wrong, fix it immediately"
5. **Show before/after** — always. The contrast tells the story.
6. **Outcome over feature** — "Know when your site drifts" not "Visual regression monitoring platform"
7. **The coding agent angle** — lean into it. "Your AI wrote code. We check the result."
8. **Price + value together** — "$19/mo. Every page, every change, full analysis."

### DON'T
- **No alarm language**: "CRITICAL", "URGENT", "WARNING" — we're watchful, not panicky
- **No generic monitoring speak**: "Anomaly detected", "Deviation identified", "Incident reported"
- **No hedging**: "might have changed", "possibly different", "could be an issue"
- **No buzzwords**: "leverage", "optimize", "cutting-edge", "AI-powered" (everyone says this)
- **No startup bro**: "crush it", "10x", "growth hacking"
- **No hand-holding**: "Don't worry, we've got you covered"
- **No emojis**. Ever.
- **No em dashes** where a comma works
- **No ALL CAPS** labels or headings
- **No mentioning specific AI models** — say "AI analysis" not "powered by Claude Opus"
- **No explaining how the analysis works** — show the output, not the process

## Copy Patterns That Work for Loupe

### The emotional arc
```
You shipped something (good for you)
→ Something on your site shifted (did you know?)
→ Here's exactly what changed (specifics)
→ Here's what it means (context)
→ Keep shipping, we'll keep watching (trust)
```

### Good headlines
- "Your site's changing. We're watching."
- "Know what your deploys did to your pages."
- "Ship fast. We'll catch what you miss."
- "Something changed on your homepage last Tuesday."

### Good CTAs
- "Audit my page"
- "Start watching"
- "See what changed"
- "Take a closer look"
- Not: "Get started", "Learn more", "Start your journey", "Try it free"

### Good trust lines
- "$19/mo. Every page, every change, full analysis."
- "Free audit. No signup. See for yourself."
- "Watching your site so you don't have to."

### The alternatives contrast
```
Check your site manually after every deploy → you won't
Ask your designer if the page looks right → they're busy (or it's you)
Stare at analytics wondering what changed → good luck
Ship with a coding agent and hope for the best → fingers crossed
```

- Ship fast and check later (or never)
- Use coding agents that make changes they don't fully review
- Know they should monitor their pages but don't have time
- Are skeptical of yet another monitoring tool
- Will pay $19/mo if we catch something real
- Value specificity over promises

## Anti-Patterns (The Bland Trap)

If your copy could appear on any monitoring tool's site by swapping the product name, it's wrong.

**Bad** (generic):
> Monitor your website for changes with AI-powered analysis. Get alerts when something changes.

**Good** (specific to Loupe):
> Your Cursor agent rewrote the pricing page. The headline's weaker, the CTA moved below the fold, and the trust badges are gone. We noticed. Did you?

**Bad** (feature-focused):
> Real-time monitoring. Multi-agent analysis. Email notifications.

**Good** (outcome-focused):
> Last Tuesday your hero text changed from "2x your conversions" to "AI-powered platform." Your headline lost its hook. We caught it.

## Context-Specific Guidelines

### Audit results page
- Lead with the strongest finding, not a score
- Be specific about what to fix and why
- This is the product demo — make it undeniably useful
- End with "Track this page" CTA

### Monitoring emails (the core product)
- Subject line = what changed: "Your homepage hero text changed"
- First line = the specific change, not "we detected a change"
- Show before/after clearly
- End with "See full analysis" (Pro upsell when applicable)

### Error messages / empty states
- Be helpful, not cute
- Tell them what to do next
- "No changes detected this week. Your page is stable." (calm, factual)

### Social / tweets
- Share real audit outputs (with permission or on own sites)
- Before/after screenshots of drift catches
- The coding agent narrative: "Your AI writes code. We check the result."
- Screenshots > descriptions
